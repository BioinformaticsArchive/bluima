In situ hybridization
Fourteen-micrometre coronal sections were cut on a cryostat,
mounted on polylysine-coated glass slides and stored at –70 °C. In
situ hybridization was performed as described by French et al. (2001).
Autoradiographs were analysed by measuring the integrated density
relative to a 14C standard using NIH Image (from NIH). Statistical
analysis was performed using the t-test. 45 mer oligonucleotides of
unique sequence were supplied by Oswel (Southampton, UK) or Life
Technologies. Sequences were:
