The normal distribution and morphology of pigmented
neurons in the human locus coeruleus (LC) is described in
detail elsewhere (German et al 1988; Chan-Palay and
Asan 1989b) and will therefore only be summarized here.
Pigmented LC neurons appear in the dorsomedial pons at
the junction between the pons and the cerebral peduncles.
